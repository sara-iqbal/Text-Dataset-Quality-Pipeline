Composite score: character entropy (35%) + word entropy (35%) + repetition (20%) + structure (10%)
COMPOSITE SCORING
Scoring Components
Character Entropy (35%)
Measures information density in the text. Low entropy = repetitive/low-quality content.
Word Entropy (35%)
Measures vocabulary diversity. Repetitive word use signals boilerplate or low-quality writing.
Repetition Ratio (20%)
Unique word count / total words. High ratio = rich, varied vocabulary.
Structure Score (10%)
Penalises excessive bullet points and markup. Rewards natural prose structure.

Known Limitations
Quality scoring is heuristic — no ground truth labels. Toxicity filter uses keyword matching, not a classifier. Language detection accuracy varies for short documents.
